Agricultural Commodities in Australia

Potato Cultivation And Dairy Production Over Time

South Australia has been a significant region for the production of potatoes as it boasts the largest plot of land between 2020-2021 but in 2022, Tasmania overtook South Australia in terms of potato production by having the largest plot of land for the production of potatoes. However, Australian Capital Territory and Northern Territory does not own any plot of land for potato production, thus making them the lowest producers in terms of potato in Australia.

Victoria has always been leading Australia in terms of Dairy Production from 1964-2022. Then, New South Wales would typically lead after Victoria and Queensland after, followed by Tasmania, Western Australia and Australian Capital Territory. However, between 1997-2008, Queensland's amount of dairy dipped, resulting in lower Dairy Production, allowing Tasmania to take their place as the second highest region in Dairy Production, having the largest increase from 1964-2022 out of every other region.

Cattle Numbers in Each Region Within Australia

While examining the total number of cattles in Australia, Queensland is shown to have the highest amount of Cattles in the region, followed by New South Wales, Victoria, Western Australia, Northern Territory, South Australia and Tasmania. However, in terms of number of cattle per square km, Victoria has the highest number of cattle per square km, followed by Tasmania, Queensland, New South Wales, Northern Territory, South Australia and Western Australia, showing the differences in Cattle Density in each region

Wheat Cultivation and Barley Production in each Region

The cultivation of wheat in Australia is always changing in each Region. Notably, Australian Capital Territory had a large plot of land to cultivate wheat in 2012 but a sharp decrease was seen in 2015. For the Northern Territory, cultivation of wheat was only possible from 2015 to 2017 as their plot of land was available in this time period. As for Tasmania, it experienced a sharp decrease in land from 2018-2019, only increasing from 2019-2022.

Barley Production varies in each region in Australia. Notably, Victoria was rank one in terms of barley production up to 1907 until South Australia took over in 1908 up to 1973 where Western Australia becomes the major producer for Barley in Australia. The only regions that does not produce barley is Australian Capital Territory and Northern Territory.
